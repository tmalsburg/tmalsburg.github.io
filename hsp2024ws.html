Project Seminar: Human Sentence Processing (WS 2024)

General information

Time: Monday, 14:00–15:30
Place: K2, M 17.25, Keplerstr. 17, second floor.
Content: This course consists of two parts: In one stream, students will be familiarized with the main research questions asked and the methods used in research on incremental human sentence processing. In the second stream, students will develop their own little experimental research studies. Concrete step-by-step guidance will be provided along the way and we will discuss progress at a series of defined milestones.
Expected knowledge: Basic linguistic theory, especially syntax, semantics, pragmatics.
Objectives: Insights into the state of the art in research on incremental human sentence comprehension. Knowledge of the basic research methods used in this area. Ability to implement a simple experimental research study.
Learning method: Presentations, discussions, tutorials, project work.
Assessment: Participation in class, presentation, project work, final report.

  • Weeks 2–5: Intro lectures will be given by myself.
  • Weeks 6–13: Student presentations, quick project updates (current milestone), guidance on what’s to do for the next milestone.

Milestone topics (tentative)

  1. Formulating a research question, selecting a suitable experimental paradigm (self-paced reading, acceptability judgments, etc.)
  2. Making stimuli: Variables that have to be controlled, comprehension questions, fillers, productive use of ChatGPT.
  3. Latin square, (pseudo)randomization, counter-balancing
  4. Implementing online experiments, instructions, consent, ethics, and common pitfalls
  5. Post-processing data and very basic data analysis
  6. Writing the research report

Presentations

  • Each student will present 1 published research study in class (approx. 25 min presentation + 10 min discussion).
  • Papers will be assigned.
  • Individual meetings to discuss draft of presentation and open questions.
    • I’m very busy, so you’ll have to make an appointment at least two weeks in advance.
    • The meeting should take place at least a week before your presentation. This way, you’ll have enough time to make adjustments in your presentation if necessary (spoiler alert: it will be necessary).
    • Bring a complete draft of your presentation and a list of open questions.
    • Meetings take place at my office (K2, R1.029) or on WebEx (less preferred).
    • In these meetings we can (and should) also discuss your project idea.

Projects

3 ECTS:

  • Formulate a research question based on the study that you presented in class.
  • Plan an experiment and prepare it up to (but not including) the data collection.
  • Write a “pre-registration” that reports all the details and the analysis plan.
  • Approx. 4 pages (not counting materials like stimuli, scripts, etc.)

6 ECTS:

  • As above, but also collect some data and report the results.
  • Approx. 6 pages (not counting materials like stimuli, scripts, etc.)

9 ECTS:

  • As above, but include a simple extension of the main experiment. Prepare the extension as a separate PCIbex experiment. However, you do not have to run the data collection and report results for the extension. It’s enough to motivate and explain the extension study and to discuss possible outcomes and their interpretation with respect to the research question that the extension is asking.
  • Approx. 8 pages (not counting materials like stimuli, scripts, etc.), i.e. two extra pages for the extension.

Project ideas and implementation

Example project ideas:

  • Take the phenomenon from the paper that you’re presenting in class but test it in your own native language.
  • Simply repeat the a study that we discussed in class if there is a reason to doubt the published result (more likely than you may think).
  • Run an improved version of a study that we discussed in class.

Implementation:

  • Research methods will typically be something like acceptability judgments and/or sentence reading times because these are relatively easy to implement.
  • Suggested software for implementing your study:
    • Google Forms: easy but no reaction times recorded.
    • SoSci Survey: still easy but more powerful and flexible.
    • PennController for IBEX: state of the art tool for psycholinguistic experiments.
